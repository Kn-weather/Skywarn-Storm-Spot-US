Skew-T Legend & How to Read

Chart Lines

Isobars — Horizontal lines of equal pressure (hPa). 1000 hPa at bottom (sea level) to 100 hPa at top (stratosphere). Labeled on the left.
Isotherms — Skewed lines of equal temperature (°C). Slant bottom-left to top-right. Labeled at the bottom. 0°C highlighted white.
Dry Adiabats — Solid brown-red curves (SE to NW). How unsaturated air cools rising (~9.8°C/km).
Moist Adiabats — Solid green-cyan curves. How saturated air cools rising (~4°C/km near surface).
Mixing Ratio Lines — Dashed green. Constant water vapor content (g/kg).
Temperature Trace — Color-coded by altitude: red (low), orange (mid), yellow (high), cyan (upper).
Dewpoint Trace — Solid green. Left of temp trace. Close = humid, far = dry.
Parcel Ascent — Dashed yellow. Path of a lifted surface parcel. Calculates CAPE/CIN/LI.
Parcel Levels: LCL (cloud base) · LFC (free convection) · EL (equilibrium)

How to Read

Y-Axis: Higher pressure = lower altitude (bottom). Lower pressure = higher altitude (top).
X-Axis: Skewed 45°. Cold left, warm right.
CAPE: Area between parcel ascent (yellow) and temp trace (red) from LFC to EL. Larger = more unstable.
CIN: Negative area below LFC. Strong cap suppresses storms.
Moisture: Gap between temp and dewpoint. Wide = dry, narrow = humid.
Wind Shear: Wind barbs on right. Check hodograph below for visual.
0°C Level: White dashed isotherm. Freezing level reference.

Hodograph

0-1 km (red) — Low-level shear. Long = tornado potential.
1-3 km (orange) — Mid-level shear.
3-6 km (yellow) — Deep-layer shear. Long = supercell potential.
6-12 km (green) — Upper-level flow.
Storm Motion (RM) — Cyan dashed. Bunkers right-mover vector.
Shape: Straight = unidirectional. Clockwise (veering) = warm advection. Counter-clockwise (backing) = cold advection.

Key Parameters

CAPE: >500 · >1500 · >2500 J/kg
LI: <0 · <-4 · <-6
Shear 0-6km: >25kt · >40kt
SRH 0-1km: >100 · >200 m²/s²
PW: >35mm heavy rain · >50mm extreme
